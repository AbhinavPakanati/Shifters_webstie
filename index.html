Our Mission

Why This Matters

Social media has become the primary source of news for millions of young adults — yet it's also the biggest breeding ground for misinformation. We set out to understand the human experience behind fake news: the confusion, the emotion, the habits that make misinformation spread.

A social media user who relies on online platforms for news needs to have a fast, simple, and trustworthy way to verify information before sharing it — because they want to stay informed quickly without accidentally spreading misinformation or damaging their credibility.

How might we help users quickly evaluate the credibility of online information so that misinformation sharing decreases and responsible digital consumption becomes a natural habit?

Solution Framing

Define Phase

Problem Statement Canvas

Using the integrated design thinking framework, we mapped our user, their context, pain points, root causes, and the scale of impact to arrive at a clear, actionable problem definition.

👤

User / Protagonist

  • Young adults aged 18–25
  • Heavy social media users
  • Platforms: Instagram, X/Twitter, TikTok, WhatsApp, YouTube
  • Consume news quickly, struggle to verify before sharing
🌐

Context & Situation

  • Viral or trending posts
  • Breaking news events
  • Emotionally charged headlines
  • AI-generated videos, images, text
  • Peer pressure to stay updated
  • Fast-paced digital environment
📋

Evidence Base

  • Users trust viral posts due to share count
  • Most do not verify before reposting
  • Fact-checking seen as time-consuming
  • AI-generated content harder to detect
  • Empathy mapping & user journey research
⚡

Pain Points & Needs

  • Difficulty identifying trustworthy information
  • Lack of quick verification tools
  • AI-generated media increasingly realistic
  • Information overload from multiple platforms
  • Fear of sharing fake information publicly
🔎

Root Causes (5 Whys)

  • Social media prioritizes engagement over accuracy
  • Users consume information too quickly
  • Fact-checking tools are disconnected from browsing
  • Emotional headlines encourage impulsive reactions
  • Social pressure encourages rapid reposting
📊

Impact & Scale

  • Rapid spread of misinformation
  • Public confusion and panic
  • Manipulation of opinions
  • Loss of trust in news
  • Damage to personal credibility
  • Increased polarization and distrust

Social media is now the dominant source of news for young adults — overtaking TV, newspapers, and official channels entirely.
Users frequently feel confused about whether news is real or fake, especially when content is emotionally charged or widely shared.
Most people rely on comments or peer validation to judge credibility — not independent fact-checking tools.
When participants realized they'd shared fake news, they reported feeling angry, embarrassed, and manipulated.
AI-generated content is making identification dramatically harder — users know it exists but lack tools to detect it.
The lack of integrated, in-platform verification tools means verification happens after sharing, not before.

🤖 The AI Amplification Problem

AI plays a significant role in worsening fake news. Since most people already lack confidence identifying misinformation, AI-generated content makes this worse by producing highly realistic articles, images, and videos indistinguishable from genuine information. AI also enables rapid, large-scale distribution through automated bots — and can manipulate social engagement through fake accounts, making false information appear credible. The result: greater confusion, stronger emotional impact, and a more profound influence on opinions and decision-making.

Emotion Arc (Score: −10 to +10)

Opportunities at Each Pain Point

📍 Stage 1: Add a quick credibility indicator to reduce information overload.
📍 Stage 2: Provide a one-click fact-check with source reliability rating.
📍 Stage 3: Show a warning prompt before sharing unverified content.
📍 Stage 4: Notify users and allow easy correction of misinformation shared.
📍 Stage 5: Encourage habit-building through rewards and verification tools.
